Thesis Project

Enhancing Patent Search, Retrieval and Comprehension using GraphRAG; A Novel Approach

This thesis proposes a novel approach to enhance patent search, retrieval, and comprehension by integrating retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) with knowledge graphs, called GraphRAG. Traditional keyword-based and Naive RAG models often miss nuanced information due to limited context handling, affecting search quality for broad queries. GraphRAG addresses these limitations by structuring patent information into knowledge graphs, thus enhancing response accuracy and contextual grounding. Evaluation shows GraphRAG significantly outperforms Naive RAG, especially for complex, global queries, offering potential advancements for patent and legal document analysis.

Technologies: Neo4j, FAISS, LangChain, OpenAI LLMs, PyPDF2
Techniques: GraphRAG, Knowledge graph construction, vector-based similarity search, community detection, prompt-based fine-tuning, query-focused summarization Skills: Natural Language Processing (NLP), semantic search, data retrieval, graph databases, generative AI
